# Project Cortex
# 03_references/reference_lighting.md

Version: 1.0

Purpose

This document defines the lighting philosophy for Project Cortex.

Lighting should reveal engineering.

It should never become the subject itself.

The objective is to make the brain appear like a premium manufactured object operating inside a futuristic laboratory rather than floating inside a game environment.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Darkness is the canvas.

Light reveals information.

Energy creates emphasis.

The lighting should communicate

Precision

Depth

Material Quality

Engineering

Every light source should have a purpose.

---

# Lighting Hierarchy

Priority

1. Electrical Emission
2. Rim Light
3. Key Light
4. Fill Light
5. Ambient Environment
6. Background

The eye should naturally follow this hierarchy.

---

# Key Light

Purpose

Reveal form.

Characteristics

- Soft
- Cool white
- Slightly directional
- Medium intensity

The key light should define the overall brain shape.

---

# Fill Light

Purpose

Prevent crushed shadows.

Characteristics

- Very soft
- Low intensity
- Neutral color

The fill light should never flatten the model.

---

# Rim Light

Purpose

Separate the brain from the background.

Characteristics

- Bright
- Cool
- Narrow
- Clean edge highlights

The rim light is critical.

Without it the brain loses depth.

---

# Ambient Environment

Purpose

Provide believable indirect illumination.

Characteristics

- Very subtle
- Neutral
- Soft reflections

The environment should support the materials without becoming noticeable.

---

# Shadow Philosophy

Shadows should feel soft.

Avoid

- Hard game-like shadows
- Completely black shadows
- Flickering shadows

Shadows should reveal depth rather than hide detail.

---

# Ambient Occlusion

Purpose

Increase perceived complexity.

AO should emphasize

- Routing channels
- Component boundaries
- Mechanical seams
- Chips
- Vias

AO should remain subtle.

Never exaggerated.

---

# Reflection Philosophy

Reflections belong only to appropriate materials.

Graphite

Minimal.

Titanium

Moderate.

Copper

Moderate.

Glass

Soft.

Electrical emission should not rely on reflections.

---

# Bloom Philosophy

Bloom represents energy.

Bloom should only appear around

- Active routing
- Electrical packets
- High-energy nodes
- Fiber optic channels

Bloom should never wash across the entire scene.

---

# Exposure

Target

Slightly dark overall exposure.

Bright emissive regions should feel valuable because the surrounding environment remains restrained.

---

# Contrast

High local contrast.

Medium global contrast.

The brain should stand out naturally without requiring excessive brightness.

---

# Color Temperature

Structural Lighting

Cool neutral.

Electrical Lighting

Defined by knowledge region colors.

Background Lighting

Slightly cooler than foreground.

Maintain a consistent visual temperature throughout the scene.

---

# Background Illumination

The background should never compete with the brain.

Allow only

- Soft gradients
- Very subtle illumination
- Minimal interface glow

No bright hotspots.

---

# HDR Environment

The HDR environment should contribute

- Soft reflections
- Material realism
- Global illumination

It should not become visually recognizable.

---

# Knowledge Region Illumination

Each knowledge region owns its own emissive color.

Inactive regions remain dim.

Active regions become brighter.

Selected regions receive the highest emphasis.

Electrical activity remains localized.

---

# Electrical Hotspots

Only a few locations should reach maximum brightness.

Examples

- Packet intersections
- Major routing hubs
- Active connectors

Avoid making every trace equally bright.

---

# Background Separation

The background should remain at least one visual level darker than the brain.

The silhouette should always be immediately readable.

---

# Lighting During Interaction

Idle

Lowest intensity.

Hover

Slight increase in emissive intensity.

Selection

Increase routing brightness.

Increase hotspot activity.

Knowledge Activation

All selected regions operate independently while preserving visual balance.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid

- Overexposed bloom
- Uniform lighting
- Flat illumination
- Rainbow lighting
- Colored shadows
- Excessive lens flare
- Bright backgrounds
- Glowing graphite

---

# Success Criteria

The lighting succeeds when

- The brain feels three-dimensional.
- Materials remain readable.
- Electrical activity naturally attracts attention.
- Depth is preserved from every viewing angle.
- The scene feels premium without relying on excessive brightness.

This document serves as the authoritative lighting reference for Project Cortex.

# Project Cortex
# 03_references/reference_materials.md

Version: 1.0

Purpose

This document defines the visual and physical material language of Project Cortex.

It is the single source of truth for every surface visible within the Engineer's Brain.

It defines appearance, not implementation.

---

# Material Philosophy

Every material should communicate precision engineering.

The brain should feel manufactured.

Nothing should appear biological.

Nothing should resemble plastic.

Every material should feel intentionally selected for its purpose.

---

# Material Hierarchy

Priority Order

1. Structural Materials
2. PCB Materials
3. Electronic Components
4. Optical Components
5. Energy Components

The eye should naturally distinguish between these layers.

---

# Material 01

## Graphite Substrate

Purpose

Primary body of the brain.

Appearance

- Dark graphite
- Matte finish
- Slight roughness
- Very subtle metallic response
- Soft ambient reflections

Characteristics

Supports every other material.

Should never emit light.

Represents the insulating PCB substrate.

---

# Material 02

## Brushed Titanium

Purpose

Mechanical shell.

Appearance

- Satin metallic finish
- Fine brushed grain
- Soft reflections
- Machined appearance

Used For

- Structural ribs
- Outer shell
- Mounting brackets
- Reinforcement

---

# Material 03

## Copper Conductors

Purpose

Electrical routing.

Appearance

- Warm metallic copper
- Semi-polished
- Slight oxidation variation
- Thin protective coating

Characteristics

Visible beneath emissive routing.

Should remain partially visible even when unlit.

---

# Material 04

## Ceramic Components

Purpose

Integrated circuits.

Appearance

- Matte ceramic
- Off-white to dark charcoal
- Fine printed markings
- Metallic pins

Examples

- ICs
- Controllers
- Processing units
- Memory packages

---

# Material 05

## Fiber Optic Channels

Purpose

Visible computational pathways.

Appearance

- Transparent protective layer
- Colored emissive core
- Glass-like surface

Behavior

Acts as the visible conduit for electrical packets.

---

# Material 06

## Tempered Glass

Purpose

Protective covers.

Appearance

- Slight transparency
- Soft reflections
- Minimal tint
- Clean edges

Used sparingly.

Never dominates the design.

---

# Material 07

## Electrical Core

Purpose

Visible computational energy.

Appearance

- Bright emissive center
- Colored outer glow
- Highest luminance in scene

Should exist only inside routing channels.

Never as large filled regions.

---

# Surface Finish

Every material should contain subtle imperfections.

Examples

- Micro scratches
- Slight roughness variation
- Tiny machining marks
- Edge wear
- Minor manufacturing inconsistencies

Avoid perfectly clean CGI surfaces.

---

# Roughness Philosophy

High Roughness

Graphite

Medium Roughness

Titanium

Low Roughness

Copper

Very Low Roughness

Glass

Emissive materials should not rely on reflections.

Their brightness comes from emission.

---

# Metallic Philosophy

Graphite

Low metallic response.

Titanium

High metallic response.

Copper

Moderately high metallic response.

Ceramic

None.

Glass

Very low metallic response.

---

# Reflection Philosophy

Reflections should support realism.

They should never become distracting.

Only metallic materials should produce noticeable reflections.

The brain should never resemble polished chrome.

---

# Color Usage

Materials remain mostly neutral.

Color belongs to energy.

Not to the structure.

The underlying engineering should remain visible even when electrical systems are inactive.

---

# Material Layering

The visual stack should read as

Mechanical Structure

↓

PCB Substrate

↓

Copper Routing

↓

Electronic Components

↓

Optical Channels

↓

Electrical Energy

Each layer should remain visually distinguishable.

---

# Material Aging

Materials should feel used but maintained.

Avoid

- Rust
- Dirt
- Heavy scratches
- Damage
- Corrosion

The object should appear operational and premium.

---

# Material Consistency

Every material must appear consistently across every lobe.

Only the emissive routing changes color.

The structural materials remain constant.

This reinforces the idea of one engineered system with multiple knowledge domains.

---

# Material Density

Different areas should exhibit different concentrations of materials.

High-complexity regions may contain

- More chips
- More routing
- More vias

Low-complexity regions should remain visually lighter.

Variation improves realism.

---

# Material Priorities

The viewer should perceive

Engineering

before

Lighting.

Materials should remain convincing even if emissive effects are temporarily disabled.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid

- Plastic appearance
- Mirror-like metals
- Fully glowing materials
- Flat color fills
- Excessively glossy surfaces
- Random material assignments
- Uniform roughness everywhere

---

# Success Criteria

The material system succeeds when

- Every surface appears manufactured.
- Structural elements remain readable.
- Electrical energy feels separate from physical materials.
- Different materials are distinguishable at a glance.
- The brain retains a premium industrial appearance under all lighting conditions.

This document serves as the authoritative material reference for Project Cortex.
